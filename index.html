Experiential Park Workshop

Cutting out templates
Creative Robotics workshop focuses on cultivating creativity and computational thinking in middle-school children while covering some STEAM concepts, by preparing them to create a model of automated amusement park. The highlight of the workshop was children creating ‘experiential rooms’ with immersive story narration and interactive games using Scratch programming. Children are making elephants from the given templates.

Programming on Scratch
We took elaborate sessions explaining them how to program on Arduino-based Hummingbird microcontroller (developed by MIT) using Scratch programming. Then students used hummingbird kit to automate the individual entities in the park. The dummy visitors are greeted by nodding elephants and are given a tour around the park in a pre-programmed car. Carousel Park and Ferris wheel are added attractions.

Experiential park in School Exhibition
Students also built a mock-up of roller coaster to experiment with potential to kinetic energy transformations. Three amusement parks were built by 3 different teams. Each team displayed two engaging stories and two interactive fun games in their experiential room. At the end, all their creations were demonstrated in the school exhibition.

Scrap Wood Storage

Building outer frames
At the Makerspace, the woodworking section needed a storage for scrap wood. The storage shelf was modelled in Solidworks and was built using scrap wood pieces collected from a nearby wood workshop. This picture shows the frames on which the rest of the shelf was built.

Fixing wheels
Wheels were attached to the bottom of the wooden slab. It was a good idea to keep the shelf easily movable as there was a doorway in front of the place where the shelf was to be kept.

Scrap wood storage
The final shelf has a separate back partition to store large piece of wood sheets and the front compartments to store small pieces. Cross links were fixed to the back partition to make it more stable.

Sewing Station

Sewing Station
The Makerspace was just starting and it had different facilities that needed a set up– woodworking section, digital fabrication section, electronics station, etc. Adding to them came the new sewing station - which was to be setup around this pillar, and I wanted to create a colourful experience for the sewers..

Sketch and brainstorming
Quick brainstorming and a rough sketch on how to improve the look.

Wall Decor
I made a patchwork on the pillar using only hexagonal shapes at the bottom to give it a neat and cohesive look. At the top, I showed the transformation of fabric at every stage on each face of the pillar.

Pillar (full view)
A full-view of the work.

Transformation of fabric
On the first two faces, I have decorated with butterflies to illustrate the source of thread. The next face has dreamcatcher that signifies thread and the last face has dress with a representation of wearables on it.

Thread stand
Also, I made a stand using wood and screws to hold sewing threads, and draped with fabric to keep the theme intact.

Shadow Box

Prototype
Shadow box was made to present as awards for best students at Gitamritam camp. Gitamritam camp is a creative, residential camp that shows youngsters how to apply teachings of Bhagavad Gita, a Hindu scripture, practically in their life. The model in this picture is the first prototype created towards this idea. I used multi-wood to provide spacing between each layer.

Shadow box
The part where Lord Krishna and Arjuna goes to war on a chariot, the introductory scene of Bhagavad Gita, was designed as different layers using Illustrator. I have used red and orange colour papers to express danger, heat and war in the air.

Lights On!
A wooden box was made to enclose the model, with its front made out of wooden frame fixed with acrylic. LED strips were used as lighting source at the back of the box. The model was placed inside the box and nails were punched through the lid to seal it.

Short Description
This artwork was created as part of the Virtual Exhibition for Art, Math, and Technology course at the University of Twente, Netherlands. The artist wants to utilize the platform to raise awareness about racism.
The current protests going on all over the world triggered a sense of fear in the artist and impelled her to come up with this artwork. Added to the consequences of COVID-19 lockdown, the death of George Floyd created turmoil in every human’s mind. This incident triggered an existential ‘fear’ in black communities. Even as authorities whose only duty is to protect people, the flaws in the judicial and social system allow colored people to be treated less than human beings. What’s scary is that we all are part of the same society and living within the same structure. This fear has been expressed through the artwork.
This work uses the audio from the original video clip of George Floyd's death circulated online.You can also navigate to the original work presented at the AMT virtual exhibition 2020 here. Do not miss reading the article on how every individual can combat racism (protest is not the only way!)

User-centered Design Workshop

Interview user needs
User-centred design workshop aims to intervene the traditional school education system in India with hands-on learning and teach creation of artefacts by fostering an understanding of its users in children. Students were given a lecture on the principles of design and each team of students was mapped to a user to create a product for their school. Children were engaged in a dialogue with the user to understand his/her needs.

Chart paper presentation
Design constraints in terms of materials were introduced to simulate a real-world experience. They had to build their products only using PVC pipes. Children presented their final idea on chart paper with a sketch of the product, its affordances, list of materials required with its specifications, after understanding user’s needs.

Build with PVC pipes
The team conducted a session on how to do measurements, use tools to cut, sand and drill and safety precautions to follow. On receiving the materials students set out to form their products.

Final creations
Children painted their products when they were finished with building it. Tests showed an increase in the children’s self-efficacy beliefs after the workshop. In this picture, all the teams are showcasing their final products joyfully.

Story telling through Sound



A narrative becomes effective when the act of telling a story is combined with various forms of media. Sound plays a crucial part in storytelling as it can express the time, place, character and evoke emotional responses in audience. To learn how to process and combine sounds efficiently I took up the course ‘Storytelling through Sound’. Several projects were to be completed to learn how to work with DAWs and the final goal is to create an immersive audio experience wherein the listener is placed at the center of the story.

First Steps: Musique concrète
While learning to work with DAWs, I created a sound composition using various sounds from sports arena, which will later be modified and manipulated. This technique is also called as Musique concrete. I recorded the sounds using Zoom H4n Pro on shuttle badminton court, volleyball field and of a bag’s opening and closing zipper. This audio also represents the transformation undergone by the sportsman in the field.

Monolgue
I added some effects to a monologue to make it dramatic. Listen to a kid chattering about what she wants to become when she grows up!

Swayamvar: The final story
My interest is in exploring a classic through new media like sound. The audio story is based on an Indian novel, the Palace of Illusions – a story that is half history and half myth. The listener can experience the events happened at Draupadi’s Swayavar. In ancient India, Swayamvar was the tradition wherein a girl of marriageable age chooses her husband from a list of suitors. On a fixed date and time, a ceremony will be held by her father, and she can choose from an assembly of suitors or the right suitor is identified on accomplishing a challenge. When the girl identifies her husband, a marriage ceremony is held immediately. Draupadi is one of the powerful women in Mahabharata Epic and her Swayawar is just announced. Let’s see what happens next and who does she marry!

Challenges faced
This project was started after the COVID19 lockdown. So one of the challenges I faced during its making was to get the right voiceovers. I requested my friends to record the dialogues and send it, however, since the recording equipment was not the same the quality of the recordings changed drastically. It was also challenging to convey the emotions in the dialogue without real-time feedbacks to the voice actors.
